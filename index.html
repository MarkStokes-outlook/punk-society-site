Why we exist

Public and private services still assume perfect memory, unlimited focus, and zero emotion. That fantasy breaks the cognitive contract for neurodivergent people. We make that failure measurable, public, and fixable.

ND truths

  • We are not broken. Systems are shipping with missing DLC.
  • Executive dysfunction is not negligence.
  • Recovery patterns matter more than punitive lockouts.
  • Evidence-backed stories beat vague accessibility statements.

The movement + the framework

Punk Society is the spotlight. It tells the stories, runs campaigns like Install the DLC, and rallies people to demand neurodivergent-friendly services.

Cognify is the standards spine. It defines the Cognitive Friction Index (CFI), publishes audits, and certifies services that genuinely work for ND communities.

Together they form the ND Audit Network: culture + rigour, memes + math.

Immediate goals

  • Build a public library of ND stories spanning 10+ sectors.
  • Run pilot audits in healthcare, government, and finance.
  • Publish the Cognify scoring playbook so allies can replicate it.
  • Fund ND testers, creatives, and organisers doing the real work.

Manifesto highlights

ND-led by default

Lived experience steers audits, research, and certification. ND contributors are paid, credited, and empowered to call out hostile patterns.

Truth over theatre

Every claim ships with receipts: reproducible steps, recordings, or scripts. If the evidence isn’t public, the work isn’t finished.

Graceful recovery

Systems must forgive missed deadlines, timeouts, and brain fog. We grade on how well you help people back on track, not how harshly you punish them.

Transparency first

Scoring maths, audit methods, and conflicts of interest stay in the open so nothing turns into corporate hand-waving.

Stories power everything

Tell us where it hurts

The Cognitive Friction Index starts with human experience. We are building an anonymous intake form, but you can start feeding the backlog today via GitHub.

Share the service, what went wrong, how it impacted you, and whether we can publish the anonymised version. We’ll route it into the ND Audit Network, prioritise sectors with the most pain, and keep you posted as audits begin.

Submit a story

Not into GitHub? Hang tight — the public form goes live soon. Mention your preferred contact channel inside the issue and we’ll follow up there.

What helps most

  • Which service, department, or flow caused the friction.
  • What you were trying to achieve and what blocked you.
  • How it affected your life (missed meds, fines, anxiety, etc.).
  • Whether we can quote you anonymously in public campaigns.
