Why we exist

Public and private services still assume perfect memory, unlimited focus, and zero emotion. That fantasy breaks the cognitive contract for neurodivergent people. We make that gap visible, offer the upgrade, and give teams the playbooks to install it.

ND truths

  • We are not broken. Systems are shipping with missing upgrades.
  • Executive dysfunction is not negligence.
  • Recovery patterns matter more than punitive lockouts.
  • Evidence-backed stories beat vague accessibility statements.

The movement + the framework

Punk Society is the spotlight. It tells the stories, maps the friction, and rallies people to demand neurodivergent-friendly services.

Cognify is the standards spine. It defines the Cognitive Friction Index (CFI), publishes audits, and certifies services that genuinely work for ND communities.

Together they form a closed loop: culture fuels demand, standards deliver proof.

Immediate goals

  • Build a public library of ND stories spanning 10+ sectors.
  • Run pilot audits in healthcare, government, and finance.
  • Publish the Cognify scoring playbook so allies can replicate it.
  • Fund ND testers, creatives, and organisers doing the real work.

Accountability stance

Grace before blame

Society isn’t broken; it’s just running the base install. We anonymise early stories, teach better patterns, and invite organisations to learn before they ever see their names in public reports.

How it works

  • Stories stay anonymous until Cognify publishes the standards and remediation paths.
  • Teams get practical guidance first, public accountability later.
  • When services refuse to upgrade, we escalate with evidence, not outrage.

Manifesto highlights

ND-led by default

Lived experience steers audits, research, and certification. ND contributors are paid, credited, and empowered to call out hostile patterns.

Truth over theatre

Every claim ships with receipts: reproducible steps, recordings, or scripts. If the evidence isn’t public, the work isn’t finished.

Graceful recovery

Systems must forgive missed deadlines, timeouts, and brain fog. We grade on how well you help people back on track, not how harshly you punish them.

Transparency first

Scoring maths, audit methods, and conflicts of interest stay in the open so nothing turns into corporate hand-waving.

Research highlights

Evidence we carry into every briefing

Punk Society translates evidence; we don’t diagnose. When we mention traits or accommodations we cite sources like the NHS, National Autistic Society, and CHADD. Full citation list lives in research/authoritative-links.md; link to it whenever you quote these highlights.

Executive function isn’t a moral issue

NHS ADHD guidance and CHADD’s time-blindness brief explain why early-morning portal races and expiring OTP codes fail ND users. Every audit should flag short timers, rigid deadlines, and “call back within 10 minutes” instructions.

Sources: NHS ADHD overview, CHADD

Sensory debt is measurable

Autistica’s sensory processing research and NAS guidance on sensory differences show why call centres, fluorescent waiting rooms, and flashing UI stack harm. Build quiet channels, predictable lighting, and the option to move interactions async.

Sources: Autistica, National Autistic Society

Demand avoidance needs collaboration

PDA Society guidance makes it clear that coercive scripts backfire for many autistic folks. Use optionality, shared planning, and restorative language instead of threats. Cross-link these recommendations to the live friction scenarios so teams see what happens when they ignore the data.

Sources: PDA Society, National Autistic Society – PDA

Stories power everything

Tell us where it hurts

The Cognitive Friction Index starts with human experience. Until the new form goes live, you can feed the backlog through GitHub Issues knowing we anonymise every story before it leaves review.

Share the service, what went wrong, how it impacted you, and whether we can quote the anonymised version later. We invite organisations to learn first, then publish names once the Cognify standard is public.

Submit a story

Not into GitHub? Hang tight — the public form goes live soon. Mention your preferred contact channel inside the issue so we can follow up privately if we need clarifications.

What helps most

  • Which service, department, or flow caused the friction.
  • What you were trying to achieve and what blocked you.
  • How it affected your life (missed meds, fines, anxiety, etc.).
  • Whether we can quote you anonymously in public campaigns.
