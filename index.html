PUNK SOCIETY

Try booking a GP appointment when you have ADHD.

You need to call at exactly 8am. If you miss it, tough luck. If you get through, they might have slots. If you don't write down the appointment time immediately, you'll forget it. If you do write it down, you'll lose the paper. Set a reminder? Sure, if you remember to set the reminder.

This isn't a personal failure. It's a system designed for people who don't exist. We're fixing it.

The problem (it's not us)

Most systems assume you have perfect memory, unlimited executive function, the ability to call places during business hours, and the mental bandwidth to navigate 12-step processes without melting down.

Cool. Except 15-20% of people don't work that way.

Energy companies make switching tariffs so complicated that people with ADHD stay on expensive rates for years. Subscription services hide the cancel button because they know you'll forget to find it. Online forms time out if you need thinking time.

These aren't edge cases. This is Tuesday.

Real examples from real people

  • GP surgeries that only take appointments by phone at 8am sharp on a Tuesday
  • Energy companies where switching tariffs is a 12-step ordeal
  • Subscription services designed to make cancellation hard to remember
  • Banks that require you to repeat security info three times across three different people
  • Online forms that time out if you need 10 minutes to think

What we're actually doing

We collect stories from neurodivergent people about where systems break. Then we show companies how to fix them.

Not with guilt. Not with public shaming. With specific, practical changes that make life easier for everyone.

Example: Instead of "be more accessible," we say: Let people book appointments online. Send confirmation texts automatically. Allow reschedules without calling. Don't make people repeat themselves.

Small changes for most people. Essential changes for us.

How this works

  • You tell us your story (anonymous is fine)
  • We find the patterns across hundreds of stories
  • We show organisations the specific fixes
  • They make changes and get certified (through our partner Cognify)
  • Everyone benefits

This isn't charity. It's system design.

This is different

Build better systems, not guilt trips

Most ND advocacy says "understand us better." We say build better systems.

We're not asking for sympathy. We're asking for online booking instead of phone-only. Text confirmations instead of "remember this." One contact person instead of explaining three times. Clear cancellation instead of dark patterns.

These help everyone. They're essential for us.

Our approach

  • Your stories stay anonymous while we find the patterns
  • We show organisations the specific, practical fixes
  • They get time to learn and make changes
  • Once companies have had time to learn, we'll call out the ones who refuse to change

Finding the patterns

Where systems break down

GP portals that time out mid-booking. Benefits forms that require 19 pieces of evidence. Energy companies that only do phone support. Banking apps that log you out if you need time to think.

These aren't random failures. They're design choices that assume everyone has perfect memory, unlimited executive function, and zero anxiety.

When enough people tell us "this system broke me," we can show organisations exactly what needs fixing.

What helps us find the patterns

  • Screenshots of timeouts, confusing interfaces, hidden cancel buttons
  • Details about what you were trying to do when it went wrong
  • Examples of what would have made it easier
  • Permission to use your anonymous story to help others

Research highlights

Evidence we carry into every briefing

Punk Society translates evidence; we don’t diagnose. When we mention traits or accommodations we cite sources like the NHS, National Autistic Society, and CHADD. Full citation list lives in research/authoritative-links.md; link to it whenever you quote these highlights.

Executive function isn’t a moral issue

NHS ADHD guidance and CHADD’s time-blindness brief explain why early-morning portal races and expiring OTP codes fail ND users. Every audit should flag short timers, rigid deadlines, and “call back within 10 minutes” instructions.

Sources: NHS ADHD overview, CHADD

Sensory debt is measurable

Autistica’s sensory processing research and NAS guidance on sensory differences show why call centres, fluorescent waiting rooms, and flashing UI stack harm. Build quiet channels, predictable lighting, and the option to move interactions async.

Sources: Autistica, National Autistic Society

Demand avoidance needs collaboration

PDA Society guidance makes it clear that coercive scripts backfire for many autistic folks. Use optionality, shared planning, and restorative language instead of threats. Cross-link these recommendations to the live friction scenarios so teams see what happens when they ignore the data.

Sources: PDA Society, National Autistic Society – PDA
